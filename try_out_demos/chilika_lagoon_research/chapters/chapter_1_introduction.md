# Chapter 1: Introduction

## 1.1 Background of Water Quality Monitoring

Water quality monitoring plays a pivotal role in sustaining aquatic ecosystems and securing human health by assessing key parameters that reflect the state of water bodies. Traditional water quality assessment methods predominantly rely on in situ sampling followed by laboratory-based chemical and biological analyses that are often labor-intensive, costly, and temporally limited. These constraints impede continuous and large-scale water quality evaluation, particularly in ecologically sensitive and extensive aquatic systems. Consequently, there has been an increasing impetus to leverage advanced technological methodologies that enable timely, comprehensive, and cost-effective monitoring solutions (MDPI, 2024; IEEE, 2024).

## 1.2 Importance of Chilika Lagoon: Ecological and Socioeconomic Significance

Chilika Lagoon, situated on the east coast of India, represents the largest coastal lagoon in the country and holds the designation of a Ramsar site of international ecological significance. It harbors rich biodiversity, supports numerous fishery livelihoods, and underpins the socioeconomic fabric of surrounding communities. The lagoon functions as a crucial habitat for migratory birds and aquatic species and sustains significant ecosystem services including nutrient cycling and shoreline stabilization. However, Chilika Lagoon faces escalating anthropogenic pressures and environmental perturbations such as eutrophication, sedimentation, and pollution, which threaten its delicate ecological balance (MDPI, 2024; NIH, 2024).

## 1.3 Challenges of Traditional Water Quality Monitoring

Conventional in situ water quality monitoring approaches in complex ecosystems like Chilika Lagoon encounter challenges related to spatial heterogeneity, temporal variability, and logistical constraints. Sampling campaigns are often constrained by accessibility, weather conditions, and financial resources, resulting in data scarcity and low temporal resolution. Additionally, in situ methods provide point-based measurements, which may not adequately capture the dynamic spatio-temporal patterns of water quality parameters across the lagoon’s heterogeneous environment. These limitations hamper effective management and timely response to ecological changes and pollution events (MDPI, 2024).

## 1.4 Role of Remote Sensing and Machine Learning in Water Quality Assessment

Recent advancements in satellite remote sensing technologies and machine learning (ML) methodologies offer transformative potentials for water quality monitoring. Satellite platforms such as Landsat 8/9 and Sentinel-2 provide multi-temporal, high-resolution imagery capable of capturing various spectral bands relevant to water quality indicators, including turbidity, chlorophyll-a concentration, and total suspended solids (TSS). Spectral indices such as the Normalized Difference Turbidity Index (NDTI), Normalized Difference Chlorophyll Index (NDCI), and Automatic Water Extraction Index (AWEI) derived from reflectance data enable estimation and spatial mapping of key water quality parameters (MDPI, 2024).

Machine learning algorithms, including Artificial Neural Networks (ANN), Random Forests (RF), and Support Vector Machines (SVM), have been integrated with remote sensing data to enhance predictive accuracy and ecological mapping. ANN models, especially multi-layer perceptrons, excel in modeling nonlinear relationships inherent in environmental data, often outperforming other algorithms in classification and regression tasks. RF algorithms offer robustness to overfitting and handle high-dimensional datasets effectively, while SVMs demonstrate strong generalization, particularly with smaller datasets and complex feature spaces. These ML approaches have advanced cost-effective, scalable, and real-time water quality monitoring frameworks suitable for dynamic ecosystems like Chilika Lagoon (MDPI, 2024; IEEE, 2024; NIH, 2024).

## 1.5 Objectives and Scope of the Review

This literature review aims to synthesize extant research on the integration of remote sensing and machine learning for water quality monitoring in Chilika Lagoon, India. It critically examines diverse satellite data sources, spectral indices, and ML algorithms applied for assessing key water quality parameters. The review evaluates the comparative performance of ML models, discusses methodological frameworks, and highlights practical applications in sustainable lagoon management. By consolidating findings from major studies conducted between 2019 and 2024, this work seeks to identify research gaps and propose future directions to enhance the precision, efficiency, and applicability of water quality monitoring technologies in Chilika Lagoon and similar coastal ecosystems.
